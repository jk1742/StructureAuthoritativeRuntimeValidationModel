Headline: across six field-level instances (S1–S3 + three S4 fields), baseline detects 3/6, proposed 6/6. S5 (refused) + T0 (accepted) show the channel gates on provenance, not value. Payload anchor: off-DOM truth carries no per-field metadata → 0.52× baseline.

L5 marks the threat-model boundary, not a measured attack. commit() and model-core.mjs are unchanged; the gate is at the event-delegation entry point.

Case 3 — Snapshot-diff vs. identity continuity Table 3

Identity continuity retains every detection the snapshot approach offers on this set, while additionally catching an identical-form replacement the snapshot misses.

ScenarioDescriptionSnapshot-diffIdentity continuity
T1identical-form replacementmisseddetected
T2genuine no-op (control)valid (no-op)valid (no-op)
T3value mutationdetecteddetected
T4structural insertiondetecteddetected
Decisive scenario T1: a new node with the same tag/attrs/value is byte-identical to the snapshot, so the diff sees no change — but it is never bound in the WeakNodeMap, so the identity is judged broken. Overhead near parity (both O(N)).

The snapshot baseline is the identity-blind minimum, not a strawman: it correctly detects T3 and T4. The two models diverge only on T1.
