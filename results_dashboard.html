Structure-Authoritative Runtime Validation — Experimental Results

Reference-implementation results for the evaluation (Section 6). Detection outcomes are deterministic and confirmed across three environments. Runtime overhead is the paired proposed/baseline ratio, reported as the median (with inter-quartile range) over 30 paired windows across 10 runs (Case 4 uses 10 warmup windows). Engines: Chromium 148.0.7778.96, Firefox 150.0.2, JSDOM on Node v22.14.0. CPU turbo disabled for clock stability. Absolute timing is environment-dependent and is not claimed.

Proposed (registry) is faster everywhere; the registry avoids embedded per-field metadata. At 2,000 nodes: 0.69× (Chromium), 0.49× (Firefox), 0.33× (JSDOM).

The baseline here is already the prior-indexed form (meta element references cached in a Map). A naive per-lookup variant, and realistic external truth stores (a signed token or IndexedDB), cost more to consult per validation — so this ratio is a conservative bound on the registry's advantage, not an exaggeration of it. JSDOM's larger margin (0.24×) reflects its slow getElementById/getAttribute and is not performance-representative; the two production engines are the headline.
